The Kidnapping of Elizabeth Smart

A Horrible Crime and an Amazing Rescue

  • Who: Elizabeth Smart, a 14-year-old girl.
  • Where: Salt Lake City, Utah.
  • When: The kidnapping happened on June 5, 2002.
  • How Long: She was missing for nine whole months.

On June 5, 2002, a man broke into Elizabeth Smart's house in the middle of the night. He had a knife and forced her to leave with him from her own bedroom. Her nine-year-old sister, Mary Katherine, was in the same room and pretended to be asleep so she wouldn't get taken too.

A massive search for Elizabeth started right away, and her face was all over the news. For months, nobody could find her. Then, Mary Katherine remembered that the kidnapper's voice sounded like a man who had done some work at their house for a day, a guy who called himself "Immanuel." This was the biggest clue in the whole case and helped the police identify the kidnapper as a man named Brian David Mitchell.

After nine long months, on March 12, 2003, people saw Elizabeth walking down the street with Mitchell and his wife, Wanda Barzee. They were only about 18 miles from her home! Even though she was wearing a wig and sunglasses as a disguise, people recognized them from TV and called the police, who came and rescued her.

Brian David Mitchell and Wanda Barzee were arrested and sent to prison for what they did. Elizabeth Smart was finally reunited with her family. Today, she is grown up and is an advocate who works to help find other missing kids and prevent abductions.

Information based on the case of Elizabeth Smart.
